Bio

My name is Chathura, and I am a final-year professional master's student at Zhejiang University. My final project focuses on leveraging AI to enhance STEM education. I completed my BEng in Chemical Engineering and Technology at Tiangong University, where I received a University Scholarship in 2022.

My academic journey was truly tested when the COVID-19 pandemic forced me to shift from an international campus to online classes from my home in Sri Lanka in 2020. Determined to supplement my virtual education with real-world experience, I joined the Joint Research and Demonstration Center for Water Technology (JRDC) as a volunteer Engineering Intern. I was later promoted to a Research Assistant and also worked as a Temporary Laboratory Instructor for the Non-Volatile Organic lab. There, I operated an HPLC instrument and a CHN analyzer, and assisted in operating an LCMS system.

My engagement with water research assisted our team to optimize an HPLC method for pesticides and study the importance of biofilm characterization in drinking water networks. This work resulted in a co-authored abstract for a conference and a special workshop program.

Working with these sophisticated instruments inspired me to learn coding to better understand the processes behind their graphical user interfaces. Interestingly, I was also inspired to pursue data analysis by the movie Moneyball, which is about baseball—my favorite sport. I was fortunate to represent my country as a teen national team player, competing in baseball championships in the United States twice.

The direction for my recent master's project was inspired by my role as a Temporary Lab Demonstrator. This experience ignited my interest in not just practicing science, but in revolutionizing how it's taught. I am currently developing another tool to learn math interactively, which you can find in my projects.
